# Glossary

## Adverse selection

The risk that you provide liquidity just before the market moves against you.

## Basis

The difference between derivative price and spot price.

## Funding rate

A periodic payment mechanism used to keep perpetual futures near spot.

## Contango

A regime where the perpetual contract trades above spot; funding is often positive.

## Backwardation

A regime where the perpetual contract trades below spot; funding is often negative.

## Liquidation

Forced position closure when margin becomes insufficient.

## Mark price

The exchange-defined fair price used for margin and liquidation calculations.

## Microprice

A short-horizon price estimate derived from bid/ask prices and sizes.

## Open interest

The total outstanding number of derivative contracts.

## Queue position

Your relative place among resting orders at the same price level.

## Ring buffer

A fixed-capacity circular buffer often used to implement fast FIFO queues.

## SPSC queue

A single-producer, single-consumer queue often used for low-latency handoff between two threads.

## Seqlock

A versioned synchronization primitive where readers retry if a writer updated the data during the read.

## Spread

The difference between best ask and best bid.

## Taker

An order that immediately removes liquidity from the book.

## Maker

An order that adds liquidity by resting on the book.
